UAT Degree Objectives — All Three Programs
# Robotics Engineering Artificial Intelligence Network Security
1 Design and complete robotic and embedded systems solutions that address real-world situations and challenges. Create and integrate cognitive artificial intelligence into applications. Create a network infrastructure design communications document that includes hardware components, connectivity, addressing, and security components.
2 Demonstrate embedded microprocessor system and circuit skills. Create and evolve natural language processing systems. Install, configure and test security hardware and software tools including port scanners, firewalls, IDS, anti-virus, and patch management.
3 Develop mechanical control systems using transducers, actuators, feedback, vision and sensing into robotic platforms. Demonstrate proficiency in designing and developing machine learning systems using industry approaches and patterns. Construct, implement and document a script or program to automate security-related processes such as administration, mapping, and auditing.
4 Examine and assess a variety of applications within the field of robotics. Design and employ deep learning systems using best practices and conventions. Create a policy or procedure addressing disaster recovery, business continuity, incident response, acceptable usage, information security, and physical security.
5 Model, analyze, and design systems that integrate hardware and software to control autonomous mechanical systems. Demonstrate a deep understanding of neural networks and their role in creating intelligent systems. Develop a research report or implementation plan concerning legal and ethical best practices for information security.
6 Implement artificial intelligence and data systems into robotic platforms. Design, create, or integrate reinforcement learning, large language models, and genetic algorithms in intelligent software. Research, document, test and evaluate industry security-based threats, encryption technologies, and mitigation techniques to protect data CIA (confidentiality, integrity, availability).
